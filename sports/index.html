THE BASICS

COMMON NAME: Llama

SCIENTIFIC NAME: Lama glama

TYPE: Mammals

DIET: Herbivores

SIZE: Height at the shoulder, 47 in

WEIGHT: 250 lbs

Llamas are pack animals that feed on grass. Llamas can survive by eating many different kinds of plants, and they need little water. These attributes make them durable and dependable even in sparse mountainous terrain.

LLAMAS VS CAMELS

So what's the deal?


The llama is actually a relative of the camel, though llamas don't have humps. They are under the same family, but different genus (#taxonomy). Camels are native to the Western and Central Asian dry deserts. They have wider feet than llamas to help them not sink in the desert sand. Llamas are often referred to as the "camels of South America". They are all about the cool, dry mountains.

And I won't even begin to differentiate between llamas and alpacas. All I know is Tony Hsieh loves llamas but owns an alpaca -- so that's confusing.
